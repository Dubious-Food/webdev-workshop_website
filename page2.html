Ingredients

Crust

  • 1/2 cups flour
  • 4 oz cream cheese
  • 6tbsp butter
  • 4-8 tbsp cold water
  • 1 tsp salt

Filling

  • 1/2 lb sweet potato*, very finely diced
  • one leek, cut in half lengthwise
  • 3/4 cup* frozen spinach/kale, thawed
  • 1/4 cup cooked bacon*, chopped
  • 4 oz cream cheese, warm
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream, room temperature
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 tsp pepper*
  • salt to taste

Instructions

Crust

  1. Combine flour, salt, cream cheese, and butter
  2. Slowly add water until dough just begins to hold together
  3. Turn onto a lightly floured silicon mat and roll into a 12 inch circle.
  4. Place dough in pie dish and crimp edges. freeze for 20 minutes
  5. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  6. Poke holes in crust with fork then cover crust with parchment or foil.
  7. bake for 12-15 minutes. Remove covering and bake for another 12-15 or until crust is dry and golden

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Over medium heat, sautee sweet potato in 1 tbsp oil until tender.
    Remove sweet potato from pan and set aside to cool. Aprox. 10-15 minutes
  3. Over medium to medium-low heat, sautee leek until translucent, stiring frequently. Aprox. 1-2 minutes
  4. Add spinach and cook off excess moisture. Remove vegetables from heat. Aprox. 2-5 minutes.
  5. Combine sweet potato, leek, spinach, and bacon with warm cream cheese.
    Add heavy cream and let cool.
  6. Combine eggs, milk, and cream cheese mixture. Add salt and pepper.
  7. Move filling to crust and bake for 20-28* minutes until center jiggles slightly and edges are set. Let cool for 20 minutes.

Notes

  • 1/2 lb sweet potato is typically equivalent to half a large sweet potato
  • I haven't tested this but you could probably use 5 oz fresh spinach instead of frozen
  • Don't use bacon grease for cooking the vegetables. That makes it too rich and makes it taste only of bacon.
  • If pepper is adding while sauteeing the vegetables, it will burn.
